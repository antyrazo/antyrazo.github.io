Media, Information, and Technology Literacy

   By definition, media literacy is the ability to access, understand, and create the various forms of media while information literacy is the ability to find, assess, and use different kinds of information. Media is also a common source of information. On the other hand, technology literacy is a person's ability to access, understand, and use technology and to look for the proper technological tools to use.

   They can affect society both positively and negatively. Through technology, many things can be done conveniently, fast, and simple. This includes the use of media to spread information. News and communication can now be accessed at the palm of people's hands. The downside of fast and simple deliverance of information is misinformation and cyberbullying can also circulate rapidly and easily. Trolls can spread propaganda through misinformation and cyberbullies can anonymously harass people because of these three.

   Media, information, and technology can also influence people's lifestyles and choices, especially young people. Many teenagers decide to go on a diet because of the models and celebrities they see on Instagram. While this can result in a healthier lifestyle, this can also lead to eating orders such as anorexia.
