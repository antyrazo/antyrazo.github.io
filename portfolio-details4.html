Media and Information Sources

   Social media and search engines like Google are the go-to sources of information by many people. However, sometimes the results are irrelevant to your needs or are inaccurate. To find accurate information that caters to your needs, there are some common tips when it comes to researching online. One is to refine your search by using other characters or terms, known as operators, to allow you to narrow down your internet search in more targeted ways. Another tip is to avoid search pitfalls by discerning facts from opinionated content.

   Other types of information sources are traditional ones like newspapers and books. Though they can be found online too nowadays, there are some websites that pose as credible ones. An example of this is when there were fake news spread by an impostor of ABS-CBN News back in 2019. With traditional printed media, there are less fake and misinformed content since there is usually a team that checks it.

   When choosing information, double-check its legitimacy and age. Information should be legitimate since the spread of misinformation and disinformation can have serious consequences. Meanwhile, the age of content matters because it might be outdated and irrelevant today. A content's publication date also matters because new information may have arisen and they weren't included because it isn't up-to-date. A good example of a search engine to use is Google Scholar since it only includes credible sources of information and you could filter the results by date.
