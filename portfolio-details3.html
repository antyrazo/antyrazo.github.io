Media Technologies

   According to the Oxford Dictionary (2022), media is a major way of receiving and distributing information and entertainment. From billboards to the internet, media has a big influence on people's lives since it can be seen everywhere we go. An example of this is social awareness. With news being accessible in a fingertips' notice, people have become more enlightened with society's issues. Victims of crime and human rights violation are now being heard because of media technologies' wider reach today.

   With all those being said, media technology has the power to transform cultures and has both positive and negative impacts. A confirmation of the said power is the popularity of cancel culture among users of social media platforms, especially Twitter. An article by Romano (2020) said that being cancelled is being "culturally blocked from having a prominent public platform or career." The article also said that cancelling someone is usually done when the words or actions by a famous person or public figure received backlash from the public (para. 1). Cancel culture has been viewed as public shaming and ostracism. Many people have regarded the movement as overcritical, censorious, unforgiving, close-minded, bullying, and toxic.

   On a positive note, cancel culture can be seen as cries of the general public for responsibility and accountability for the rich and powerful. In my opinion, cancel culture can be helpful if it is not overly used in order to lessen toxicity in social media environments. It has also been considered by some as a consequence of a figure's actions. A Forbes article (Brownlee, 2021) compared cancelling someone to consequences:

But on the other hand, much of what seems to be increasingly labeled as "cancel culture" may just be long overdue accountability for the elite and powerful who have grown accustomed to the tacit immunity that often accompanies wealth, power and celebrity. For people of color and other marginalized communities, our "cancel culture" was always in effect only then, the powers that be labeled it "consequences." (para. 5)

   An example of people being cancelled on Twitter in the Philippines are Angel Locsin and Toni Gonzaga. Recently, Filipino netizens compared the cancellation of the two by pointing out that Locsin was cancelled for being an attention-grabber because she was helping hungry Filipinos during the pandemic. Meanwhile, Gonzaga was cancelled for publicly voicing her support and influencing people to vote a convicted politician despite her being known to uphold Christian values. This comparison shows that not only do media technologies have the power to transform cultures through influence and cancel culture, but it also shows that it has its pros and cons.
